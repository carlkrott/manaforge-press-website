In the Caelus Universe, void technology keeps humanity alive — and drives them mad.

This is the setting of *Echoes of Caelus*, D.F.Bruno's ongoing series that blends LitRPG progression mechanics with cosmic horror and corporate dystopia. The first book, *VoidWalker*, follows Jaxson — a maintenance worker on a dying station who discovers what AetherCorp has been hiding in the dark spaces between dimensions.

## Void Technology

Void energy is the backbone of civilisation in Caelus. It powers faster-than-light travel, advanced computation, and limited reality manipulation. Every station, every ship, every corporate facility runs on it.

But the void gives nothing freely.

Prolonged exposure causes psychological decay. Physical transformation. And worst of all — the hungry attention of incomprehensible entities lurking beyond the veil. These are not gods or demons in any traditional sense. They are something else entirely. Something that notices when you look at it.

## AetherCorp

The megacorporation that owns the void. Owns the stations. Owns the supply chains that keep humanity breathing recycled air in the black. AetherCorp is not evil in the mustache-twirling sense — it is something worse. It is efficient. It is bureaucratic. It treats human lives as line items on a quarterly report.

When Jaxson discovers the truth about what AetherCorp extracts from the void — and what they're willing to sacrifice to keep extracting it — the corporation's response is not anger. It is a cost-benefit analysis.

## The Blue-Collar Underdog

What makes *VoidWalker* different from standard space opera is its protagonist. Jaxson fixes pipes. He replaces filters. He is the most expendable person on the station — until he becomes the only person who can see what's coming through the cracks in reality.

This is progression fantasy set in a science fiction world. The power system is tied to void exposure — the more you interact with the space between dimensions, the more you change. But change is not the same as growth. Every surge of power comes with a cost that Jaxson may not be able to pay.

> The void doesn't just take. It changes you.
